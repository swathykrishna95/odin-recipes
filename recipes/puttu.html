Puttu Recipe

Description

The puttu is a very famous Kerala dish that is basically steamed rice flour sandwiched by shredded coconut. It is made is a special vessel thst is shaped as a cylinder. Other flour types also may be used, such as wheat, sooji etc.

Ingredients

  • Puttu flour
  • Salt
  • Water
  • Coconut(shredded)

Steps

  1. Take the Puttu flour into a bowl.
  2. Add salt as required.
  3. Add water and mix with your fingers till the flour forms lumps.
  4. Break all the lumps and make the mix even.
  5. Boil 2-3 cups of water in a puttu kodam(vessel).
  6. Keep the perforated disk inside the cylinder and add an even layer of shredded coconut over it.
  7. Fill the cylinder upto half with the mixed flour.
  8. Add one more layer of the shredded coconut. Make sure it is even.
  9. Fill the flour upto the brim and add a final layer of coconut.
  10. Close the vessel and keep it on top of the puttu kodam, which must be boiling by now.
  11. Let it cook on a medium flame till the whistle comes. Then remove the puttu from the vessel with the help of a stick.
  12. Serve on a plate with hot Kadala Curry!
